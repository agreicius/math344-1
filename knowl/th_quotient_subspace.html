Theorem 1.11.9. Quotients and subspaces.

Let \(p\colon X\rightarrow Y\) be a quotient map, and let \(A\subseteq X\) be a subspace that is saturated with respect to \(p\text{.}\) Let \(q=p\vert_A\colon A\rightarrow p(A)\) be the restriction of \(p\) to \(A\text{.}\)
  1. If \(A\) is either open or closed, then \(q\) is a quotient map.
  2. If \(p\) is either an open or a closed map, then \(q\) is a quotient map.
