Definition 1.2.1. Topology generated by a collection of subsets.

Let \(X\) be a set, and let \(\mathcal{A}\subseteq\mathcal{P}(X)\) be a collection of subsets of \(X\text{.}\) The topology generated by \(\mathcal{A}\) is the coarsest topology \(\mathcal{T}\) on \(X\) containing \(\mathcal{A}\text{.}\) In more detail, \(\mathcal{T}\) is the unique topology on \(X\) satisfying the following:
  1. \(\mathcal{A}\subseteq \mathcal{T}\text{.}\)
  2. If \(\mathcal{T}'\) is a topology and \(\mathcal{A}\subseteq \mathcal{T}'\text{,}\) then \(\mathcal{T}\subseteq \mathcal{T}'\text{.}\)
