Let \(I\) be a set. Given a set \(X\text{,}\) an \(I\)-tuple of elements of \(X\) is a function \(\boldx\colon I\rightarrow X \text{.}\) Given an \(I\)-tuple \(\boldx\) and element \(i\in I\) we will often denote the value \(\boldx(i)\) as \(x_i\text{,}\) and denote \(\boldx\) itself as \(\boldx=(x_i)_{i\in I}\text{.}\) In analogy to finite tuples, we call \(x_i\) the \(i\)-th entry or coordinate of \(\boldx\text{.}\)
