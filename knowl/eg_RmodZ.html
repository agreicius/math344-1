Example 1.11.9. The circle as a quotient space.

Let \(X^*\) be the quotient of \([0,1]\) obtained by identifying only the elements \(0\) and \(1\text{.}\) Let \(\R/\Z\) denote the quotient of \(\R\) by the equivalence relation \(x\sim y\) if and only if \(x=y+n\) for some \(n\in Z\text{.}\)
  1. Prove: \(X^*\) and \(\R/\Z\) are homeomorphic.
  2. Prove: \(\R/\Z\) is homeomorphic to \(S^1=\{(x,y)\colon x^2+y^2=1\}\text{.}\)
