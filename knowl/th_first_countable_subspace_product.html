Theorem 1.18.7. First countable: subspaces, images, and products.

  1. If \(X\) is first countable and \(Y\subseteq X\) is a subspace, then \(Y\) is first countable.
  2. If \(f\colon X\rightarrow Y\) is open, continuous and surjective, then if \(X\) is first countable, \(Y\) is first countable.
  3. Let \(X=\prod_{i\in I}X_i\) be a product of topological spaces. The following statements are equivalent.
    1. \(X\) is first countable.
    2. \(X_i\) is first countable for all \(i\in I\) and the set \(J\) of all \(j\in I\) such that \(X_j\) does not have the trivial topology is countable.
    As a result, a countable product of first countable spaces is first countable.
