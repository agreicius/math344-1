Example 1.7.7. Convergent sequences in \(\R^I\).

Let \(X=\R^I\text{,}\) where \(I\) is an infinite set, let \(f=(x_i)\in X\text{,}\) and let \((f_n)_{n=1}^\infty\) be a sequence of elements of \(X\text{.}\) We will compare convergence and pointwise convergence of this sequence with respect to the box and product topologies on \(X\text{,}\) where \(\R\) is equipped with the standard topology.
  1. Prove: for both the box and product topologies, if \(f_n\rightarrow f\) for some \(f\in \R^I\text{,}\) then the \(f_n\) converge pointwise to \(f\text{.}\)
  2. Prove: \(f_n\) converges to \(f\) in the product topology if and only if \(f_n\) converges to \(f\) pointwise.
  3. Let \(X=\R^{\omega}\text{.}\) For all \(n\in \Z^+\text{,}\) define \(f_n\in X\) as
    \begin{equation*} f_n=(1/n, 1/n,\dots)\text{.} \end{equation*}
    Prove: \(f_n\) converges to \((0,0,\dots)\in X\) pointwise, but does not converge to any element of \(X\) with respect to the box topology.
